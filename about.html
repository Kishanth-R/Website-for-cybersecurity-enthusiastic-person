What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These attacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information, extorting money from users, or interrupting normal business processes.

History of Cybersecurity

The field of cybersecurity has evolved significantly since the 1970s. Early efforts focused on protecting military communications, but with the rise of the internet in the 1990s, cybersecurity became crucial for businesses and individuals alike. Major milestones include the creation of the first antivirus software and the establishment of organizations like CERT.

Importance of Cybersecurity

In today's digital world, cybersecurity is essential to protect against threats like data breaches, ransomware, and identity theft. It ensures the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information, safeguarding personal data, financial systems, and national infrastructure.

Types of Cybersecurity

  • Network Security: Protects network infrastructure from unauthorized access.
  • Application Security: Focuses on keeping software and devices free of threats.
  • Information Security: Protects the integrity and privacy of data.
  • Operational Security: Involves processes and decisions for handling and protecting data.
  • Disaster Recovery: Plans for responding to security breaches and restoring systems.

Common Threats

Cyber threats include malware, phishing, DDoS attacks, and insider threats. Understanding these is key to effective defense.
